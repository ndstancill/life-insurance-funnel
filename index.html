Will this affect my credit or run a hard check?
Quotes do not impact credit. Some carriers may do a soft check and routine records lookups (RX/MIB) during underwriting—these aren’t hard pulls and won’t hurt credit.
What’s the difference between term and permanent life insurance?
Term (10–30 years) gives the highest coverage per dollar and is great for income/mortgage protection. Permanent (whole or universal) can last for life and may build cash value. I’ll help you pick the fit for your budget and goals.
Do I need a medical exam?
Many “accelerated” options in my toolkit don’t require a medical exam if you qualify. If an exam is needed, it’s usually a quick, free visit and we’ll schedule it around you.
How much coverage should I choose?
Common starting points: 10–12× annual income or enough to cover your mortgage, debts, and 5–10 years of living costs. I’ll run side-by-side options so you can choose what feels right.
How fast can coverage start?
Same-day approvals are possible with some carriers. Others take a few days to a couple of weeks depending on underwriting.
What if I already have life insurance?
We’ll review what you have and see if adding or replacing coverage makes sense. I’ll never suggest canceling anything until a new policy is approved and active.
