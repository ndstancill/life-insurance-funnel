How Much Life Insurance Do You Really Need?

A quick rule of thumb: many families aim for 10–15× annual income in total coverage. That’s usually enough to:

  • Pay off the mortgage or keep the family in the house
  • Replace several years of income for your spouse
  • Help kids with college and major milestones

But rules of thumb don’t tell the whole story. I walk through your actual numbers to make sure the coverage fits your real life—not a generic formula.

Term vs Whole Life: Which Makes Sense for You?

When Term Life Makes Sense

Term is usually best when your top priority is maximizing death benefit per dollar: mortgages, income replacement, and kids at home. It’s often the ideal “first policy” for most families.

When Permanent Coverage Helps

Permanent coverage (whole life, IUL, etc.) can make sense for final expenses, legacy planning, or specific tax and cash-value strategies. Often, a mix of term and permanent coverage is most efficient.

Common Life Insurance Questions

Do I have to talk to a call center?

No. When you call or submit the form, you work directly with me, Dail—not a national call center or rotating agent pool. You’ll have one point of contact from quote to approval.

Can you help if I’ve been declined before?

Yes. Different carriers look at health conditions in different ways. I can review what happened and target companies that are more friendly to your specific situation.

What does it cost to use you as my agent?

You never pay me a separate fee. I’m compensated by the carriers when a policy is issued and in force. Your premium would be the same if you went direct to the company.

What if I’m not sure how much I can afford?

That’s normal. We can start with a basic term policy that fits comfortably, then layer coverage or adjust later as your income and goals change. Some coverage is almost always better than none.
